# Stylistic Tells - patterns to find and rewrite

Purpose: filings must read as competent, human legal writing. These patterns undermine credibility whether they come from an AI draft or an inexperienced writer. This is about craft, not concealment - if the court has an AI-disclosure standing order (see case-profile.md), the user must comply with it.

## Machine-drafting tells
- Em dashes used as the default connector (use en dashes or restructure)
- "It is important to note that", "It should be noted", "Notably," as filler openers
- Triadic padding: "clear, consistent, and compelling" strings with no distinct meaning per word
- "Moreover/Furthermore/Additionally" opening consecutive paragraphs
- Hedged conclusions: "may potentially", "could arguably suggest"
- Perfectly parallel bullet lists where prose argument is expected
- Summarizing headers restating the sentence below them
- Uniform paragraph lengths; no sentence-length variation
- Over-formal thesaurus words: "utilize", "endeavor", "aforementioned", "herein" sprinkled without legal function
- Concluding paragraphs that restate everything just said
- Fabrication-pattern citations: string cites with suspiciously on-point parentheticals - always verified in pass 1, but treat as a red flag here too

## Amateur / pro se tells
- Narrative autobiography instead of numbered factual allegations
- Legal conclusions in fact sections ("Defendant breached the contract" as a "fact")
- Citing Wikipedia, blogs, or secondary summaries instead of primary authority
- Quoting entire statutes instead of the operative clause
- Addressing the judge conversationally ("Your Honor, you have to understand...")
- Underlining + bold + italics stacked for emphasis

## Rewrite principles
- Short declarative sentences carry legal writing. Vary length for rhythm, but default to direct.
- One idea per paragraph, topic sentence first.
- Facts persuade; adjectives confess weakness. Delete the adjective, keep the fact.
- Active voice with named actors ("Smith sent the notice on May 3", not "the notice was sent").
- Every paragraph should survive the question: what does this make the judge do?
